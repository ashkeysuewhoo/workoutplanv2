🌞 Warm-up every session
  • 5 min light cardio (bike or treadmill)
  • Arm circles, leg swings, hip openers
  • 2 warm-up sets on your first exercise
🌙 Cool-down every session
  • 5 min easy walk on the treadmill
  • Hold each stretch 30–45 seconds
  • Focus on the muscles you just worked
📈 Progressive Overload
  • Add 2.5–5 lbs when you hit top of rep range
  • Write down your working weights each session
  • Form first, weight second — always!
💫 Body Recomp Reminder
  • Eat near maintenance — small deficit is fine
  • High protein is your best friend 🥩
  • Results are slower but they STICK!

💕 Key Nutrition Rules for Recomp
  • Protein with every meal — chicken, fish, eggs, beef, yogurt, cottage cheese
  • Time your carbs around workouts for energy & recovery

💡Beginner Tips🌺
Little things that make a big difference 💕
🛁
Don't skip the "boring" stuff
Warm-up, cool-down, and sleep are not optional extras — they're literally where your body adapts and changes. Most beginners skip these and wonder why they plateau after 8 weeks!
🪞
Form first, weight second always
Bad form + heavy weight = injury. Injury = weeks off = zero progress. Learn the movement with light weight first, then load it up. Use a mirror or your phone camera to check yourself!
📸
The scale is a liar — take progress photos!
During recomp, you can lose fat AND gain muscle at the same time, so the scale might barely move while your body totally transforms. Take photos every 4 weeks and measure your waist — that's the real tea ☕
🌷
Consistency beats perfection every time
A good workout done consistently beats a perfect workout done twice a month. Missed a day? Don't double up — just pick up where you left off. One missed session = nothing. Missing every session = everything.
😌
Soreness ≠ a good workout
As your body adapts, you'll feel less sore — and that's actually a GOOD sign, not regression! Chase progressive overload (lifting more over time), not soreness. Your muscles are learning 🧠
🥩
Protein is truly non-negotiable
Without enough protein, your body literally can't repair and build muscle — even if you train perfectly. Struggling to hit your target from food alone? A protein shake is a totally valid and easy fix 🥤
💪
Week 4–6 is when most people quit — don't!
The new-gym excitement wears off but visible results haven't fully shown yet. This is the critical window! The girls who push through this phase are the ones who actually glow up. You WILL see the results — trust the process 🌸
